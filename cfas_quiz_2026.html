Problem 1 PAS 2 — Inventories (FIFO & Weighted Average)
Magaling Trading Co. provided the following inventory data for the year ended December 31, 2025:

Jan 1 — Beginning inventory: 100 units @ ₱50.00
Mar 15 — Purchase: 300 units @ ₱55.00
Jul 20 — Purchase: 200 units @ ₱62.00
Sep 5 — Sale: 420 units
Dec 31 — Ending inventory: 180 units (verified by count)

Required:
(a) Cost of ending inventory and Cost of Goods Sold using FIFO
(b) Cost of ending inventory and Cost of Goods Sold using Weighted Average Cost
SOLUTION — PAS 2: INVENTORIES ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ TOTAL UNITS AVAILABLE: 100 + 300 + 200 = 600 units Units sold: 420 | Ending inventory: 180 units ✓ (600 − 420 = 180) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ (a) FIFO — First-In, First-Out COGS (420 units sold — earliest costs first): From Jan 1: 100 units × ₱50.00 = ₱ 5,000 From Mar 15: 300 units × ₱55.00 = ₱ 16,500 From Jul 20: 20 units × ₱62.00 = ₱ 1,240 ───────────────────────────────────────────── Total COGS: 420 units = ₱ 22,740 ENDING INVENTORY (remaining 180 units — latest costs): From Jul 20: 180 units × ₱62.00 = ₱ 11,160 ───────────────────────────────────────────── FIFO Ending Inventory = ₱ 11,160 PROOF: ₱22,740 + ₱11,160 = ₱33,900 (Total TGAS) ✓ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ (b) WEIGHTED AVERAGE COST METHOD Total Cost of Goods Available for Sale (TGAS): 100 × ₱50.00 = ₱ 5,000 300 × ₱55.00 = ₱16,500 200 × ₱62.00 = ₱12,400 ───────────────────────── TGAS: 600 units = ₱33,900 Weighted Average Unit Cost: ₱33,900 ÷ 600 units = ₱56.50 per unit COGS: 420 units × ₱56.50 = ₱23,730 ENDING INVENTORY: 180 units × ₱56.50 = ₱10,170 PROOF: ₱23,730 + ₱10,170 = ₱33,900 ✓ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ KEY CONCEPTS TESTED: • LIFO = PROHIBITED under PAS 2 • FIFO: ending inventory = most recent costs • WA: single average cost applied to all units • Always verify: COGS + Ending Inv = TGAS
Problem 2 PAS 7 — Statement of Cash Flows (Indirect Method)
Daluyong Corporation reported the following for year ended December 31, 2025:

Net profit after income tax: ₱1,500,000
Depreciation expense: ₱200,000
Amortization of intangible assets: ₱50,000
Gain on sale of equipment: ₱80,000
Loss on sale of investments: ₱30,000
Increase in accounts receivable: ₱120,000
Decrease in inventories: ₱60,000
Increase in accounts payable: ₱90,000
Decrease in accrued liabilities: ₱25,000
Decrease in income tax payable: ₱40,000

Required: Compute the Net Cash from Operating Activities using the Indirect Method.
SOLUTION — PAS 7: STATEMENT OF CASH FLOWS (INDIRECT METHOD) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ DALUYONG CORPORATION Cash Flows from Operating Activities (Indirect Method) For the Year Ended December 31, 2025 Net profit after income tax ₱1,500,000 Adjustments for non-cash items: Add: Depreciation expense 200,000 Add: Amortization of intangibles 50,000 Less: Gain on sale of equipment (80,000) ← remove; belongs in investing Add: Loss on sale of investments 30,000 ← add back; belongs in investing Changes in working capital: Less: Increase in accounts receivable (120,000) ← cash not yet received Add: Decrease in inventories 60,000 ← sold for cash Add: Increase in accounts payable 90,000 ← not yet paid in cash Less: Decrease in accrued liabilities (25,000) ← paid in cash Less: Decrease in income tax payable (40,000) ← paid more cash ────────── Net Cash from Operating Activities ₱1,665,000 ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ RULES TO REMEMBER (PAS 7): • Non-cash EXPENSES (depreciation, amortization) → ADD BACK • Gains on disposal → DEDUCT (will be in investing section) • Losses on disposal → ADD (will be in investing section) • Increase in current ASSET → DEDUCT • Decrease in current ASSET → ADD • Increase in current LIABILITY → ADD • Decrease in current LIABILITY → DEDUCT
Problem 3 PAS 12 — Income Taxes (Current & Deferred)
Buwis Corporation provided the following for the year ended December 31, 2025:

Accounting profit before income tax: ₱5,000,000
Items causing differences from taxable profit:
  (a) Non-deductible entertainment expenses (permanent): ₱200,000
  (b) Depreciation per books > Tax depreciation by ₱400,000 (taxable temporary difference — tax deducted MORE this year)
  (c) Warranty expense accrued in books ₱300,000 (deductible for tax only when actually paid — deductible temp. difference)
Corporate income tax rate: 25%

Required:
(a) Taxable profit and current tax expense
(b) Deferred tax expense or income
(c) Total income tax expense in profit or loss
SOLUTION — PAS 12: INCOME TAXES ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ (a) TAXABLE PROFIT AND CURRENT TAX Accounting profit before tax ₱5,000,000 Permanent difference: Add: Non-deductible entertainment 200,000 ← deducted in books, never allowed for tax Temporary differences: Less: Extra tax depreciation (400,000) ← tax deducted MORE → taxable profit lower Add: Warranty expense (not yet deductible) 300,000 ← accrued in books, not yet deductible for BIR ────────── TAXABLE PROFIT ₱5,100,000 CURRENT TAX EXPENSE: ₱5,100,000 × 25% = ₱1,275,000 ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ (b) DEFERRED TAX MOVEMENTS Item (b) — Extra tax depreciation ₱400,000: Tax base of asset < Carrying amount → TAXABLE temp. diff. → Deferred Tax LIABILITY (DTL) = ₱400,000 × 25% = ₱100,000 (Will pay MORE tax in the future when tax depreciation runs out) Item (c) — Warranty accrual ₱300,000: Tax base of liability = ₱0 (not yet deductible); CA = ₱300,000 → DEDUCTIBLE temp. diff. → Deferred Tax ASSET (DTA) = ₱300,000 × 25% = ₱75,000 (Will save on tax in the future when warranty is actually paid) Net Deferred Tax EXPENSE = DTL ₱100,000 − DTA ₱75,000 = ₱25,000 (net DTL) ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ (c) TOTAL INCOME TAX EXPENSE Current tax expense ₱1,275,000 Deferred tax expense (net) 25,000 ────────── TOTAL INCOME TAX EXPENSE ₱1,300,000 PROOF CHECK: (Accounting profit + permanent diff.) × tax rate = (₱5,000,000 + ₱200,000) × 25% = ₱1,300,000 ✓ ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ KEY RULES (PAS 12): • Permanent diff. → affects CURRENT TAX only; NO deferred tax • Taxable temp. diff. → DTL (pay more later) • Deductible temp. diff. → DTA (pay less later) • Deferred taxes = NEVER DISCOUNTED; always NONCURRENT
Problem 4 PAS 16 — Property, Plant and Equipment (Revaluation Model)
Haligi Corporation acquired a building on January 1, 2023 for ₱6,000,000. Estimated useful life: 20 years; residual value: ₱0; straight-line method. The company uses the Revaluation Model.

On December 31, 2024 (after 2 full years), the building is revalued. The appraised fair value is ₱5,800,000.

On December 31, 2026 (after 2 more years), the building is revalued again. The appraised fair value is ₱4,200,000.

Required:
(a) Carrying amount immediately BEFORE the December 31, 2024 revaluation
(b) Entry to record the December 31, 2024 revaluation
(c) Carrying amount immediately BEFORE the December 31, 2026 revaluation
(d) Entry to record the December 31, 2026 revaluation and where losses are charged
SOLUTION — PAS 16: PPE REVALUATION MODEL ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ ORIGINAL COST (Jan 1, 2023) ₱6,000,000 Useful life: 20 years; Annual depreciation: ₱6,000,000 ÷ 20 = ₱300,000 ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ (a) CARRYING AMOUNT BEFORE DEC 31, 2024 REVALUATION Cost: ₱6,000,000 Accumulated depreciation: (₱300,000 × 2 years) = (₱600,000) Carrying amount (Dec 31, 2024): ₱5,400,000 (b) REVALUATION — DEC 31, 2024 Fair value: ₱5,800,000 Carrying amount before: ₱5,400,000 REVALUATION INCREASE: ₱ 400,000 → OCI (Revaluation Surplus) JOURNAL ENTRY (elimination method): Accumulated Depreciation ₱600,000 Building (to fair value) ₱200,000 (net: 5,800 − 6,000 = −200 to cost) Wait — net effect: Building restated to ₱5,800,000. Using ELIMINATION method: Dr. Accumulated Depreciation 600,000 Cr. Building 200,000 Cr. Revaluation Surplus (OCI) 400,000 New CA = ₱5,800,000 (no accumulated depreciation after revaluation) Remaining useful life = 18 years New annual depreciation = ₱5,800,000 ÷ 18 = ₱322,222 ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ (c) CARRYING AMOUNT BEFORE DEC 31, 2026 REVALUATION Carrying amount after Dec 31, 2024 revaluation: ₱5,800,000 Depreciation 2025 + 2026: (₱322,222 × 2) = (₱644,444) ────────── Carrying amount (Dec 31, 2026) BEFORE revaluation: ₱5,155,556 (d) REVALUATION — DEC 31, 2026 Fair value: ₱4,200,000 Carrying amount before: ₱5,155,556 REVALUATION DECREASE: ₱ 955,556 ALLOCATION OF DECREASE: Against existing Revaluation Surplus: ₱400,000 → debit OCI (Revaluation Surplus) Excess charged to Profit or Loss: ₱555,556 → debit P&L (Impairment/Revaluation loss) JOURNAL ENTRY: Dr. Revaluation Surplus (OCI) 400,000 Dr. Revaluation Loss (P&L) 555,556 Dr. Accumulated Depreciation 644,444 Cr. Building 1,600,000 ━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━ KEY RULES (PAS 16 REVALUATION): • Revaluation INCREASE → OCI (Revaluation Surplus) • Revaluation DECREASE → first reduce Revaluation Surplus; excess to P&L • After revaluation: recalculate depreciation based on new CA and remaining life • Revaluation Surplus may be transferred to Retained Earnings as asset is used
